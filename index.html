Projects

Quantitative Development Intern

Astignes Capital Asia Pte. Ltd.

May 2022 - Aug 2022


Implemented a novel risk regime indicator using timeseries data. Experimented with methods such as Hidden Markov Models and Gaussian Mixture Models to quantify regime changes and its implications for risk.

Translated regime information into trading signals by using Genetic Algorithms (GA). GA was used to explore a large (325) parameter space to obtain an optimal set of heuristics.

Designed, maintained, and deployed an in-house dashboard in Plotly Dash for data visualisation with automatic daily updates, enhancing user interactivity and data exploration.

Seismology and Machine Learning for the Aceh region in Indonesia

GitHub

Dec 2020 - May 2022


Tested and retrained a recently published CNN, EQTransformer, on a new ~100 TB dataset from Aceh, Indonesia, with the goal of micro-earthquake detection, and modelling of the seismic properties of this highly active region.

Performed earthquake association and relocation, and verified the robustness of EQTransformer detections through bootstrapping and Monte Carlo methods. Conducted descriptive statistics on the seismicity and magnitude-frequency distribution of this region, setting the groundwork for future fault modelling.

Developed a Python pipeline for use with a super-computing cluster, along with data processing and plotting tools.

Designing a Cell-Counting Microscope

GitHub   Project Blog

May - Jul 2021



Designed and tested a cell counting microscope, with mechanical motion control and automated focusing. Cell counting was accomplished using the OpenCV library. Wrote an accompanying web interface in Flask. Tested and experimented with a TensorFlow CNN model, though it turned out that blob detection was more performant.

Our pipeline achieved ~90% accuracy in both synthetic and real-life tests.

Science Research Programme @ National University of Singapore

2016


Solo project to build a grating spectrometer with sub-nanometre resolution, for the purposes of characterising the spectra produced by Avalanche Photodiode flash breakdown, capable of temporally resolving single photons. This has implications for the security of Quantum Key Distribution schemes.

Presented at the Institute of Physics, Singapore (IPS) Meeting 2017, and Singapore Science and Engineering Fair 2017.
