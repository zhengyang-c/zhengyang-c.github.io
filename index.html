Senior Honours Project

Nanyang Technological University
School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences

June 2023 - Present



Investigating and proposing a modification to models of neutrino mass generation

Since the 5Ïƒ detection of the Higgs boson in 2012, the Standard Model of Physics was completed. Although it is the most predictive theory in physics to date, there are reasons to believe that it is incomplete. Neutrinos are extremely light and weakly interacting particles. However, the Standard Model predicts that neutrinos are massless. Thus, new physics beyond the Standard Model is required to explain why neutrinos have mass.

In 1975, an extension named the Left-Right Symmetric Model (LRSM) was proposed by Mohapatra et al., addressing the aesthetic limitations of the Standard Model by proposing a deeper symmetry of nature. This also offers new avenues and directions from which we can propose a neutrino mass generation mechanism.

This theoretical project in Quantum Field Theory hence aims to study how the LRSM is able to generate finite neutrino masses, before proposing extensions to current models.

Seismology and Machine Learning for the Aceh region in Indonesia

Earth Observatory Singapore

Dec 2020 - May 2022


GitHub
Tested and retrained a recently published CNN, EQTransformer, on a new ~100 TB dataset from Aceh, Indonesia, with the goal of micro-earthquake detection, and modelling of the seismic properties of this highly active region.

Performed earthquake association and relocation, and verified the robustness of EQTransformer detections through bootstrapping and Monte Carlo methods. Conducted descriptive statistics on the seismicity and magnitude-frequency distribution of this region, setting the groundwork for future fault modelling.

Developed a Python pipeline for use with a super-computing cluster, along with data processing and plotting tools.

Designing a Cell-Counting Microscope

Nanyang Technological University, School of Physical and Mathematical Sciences

May - Jul 2021


GitHub   Project Blog
Designed and tested a cell counting microscope, with mechanical motion control and automated focusing. Cell counting was accomplished using the OpenCV library. Wrote an accompanying web interface in Flask. Tested and experimented with a TensorFlow CNN model, though it turned out that blob detection was more performant.

Our pipeline achieved ~90% accuracy in both synthetic and real-life tests.
